Components of a Robotic Arm Joints and Links: The robotic arm consists of multiple segments (links) connected by joints (revolute or prismatic), allowing various degrees of freedom (DOF).
End-Effector: The tool attached to the end of the robotic arm, which interacts with the environment. It can be a gripper, welder, vacuum, or any other tool specific to the task.
Actuators: Motors (electric, hydraulic, or pneumatic) that drive the joints, enabling movement.
Sensors: Provide feedback on position, force, torque, and other parameters to ensure precise control.
Controller: The computer system that directs the arm's movement and processes feedback from sensors. Power Supply: Provides the necessary energy for the actuators and sensors.

Articulated Robots: Mimic the human arm with multiple rotating joints. They offer high flexibility and are widely used in manufacturing.
SCARA (Selective Compliance Articulated Robot Arm): Features a rigid vertical axis and flexible horizontal axes, ideal for pick-and-place tasks.
Delta Robots: Consist of parallelograms connected to a common base, known for high speed and precision in light assembly tasks.
Cartesian Robots: Operate on three linear axes (X, Y, Z), providing a rectangular work envelope and high precision in linear movements.
Cylindrical Robots: Combine rotary and linear motion, suitable for assembly operations requiring a cylindrical work envelope.

Manufacturing: Widely used for assembly, welding, painting, and material handling, enhancing production speed and quality.
Healthcare: Assist in surgeries (e.g., da Vinci Surgical System), rehabilitation, and laboratory automation.
Logistics: Automate sorting, packing, and palletizing tasks in warehouses.
Agriculture: Used for planting, harvesting, and monitoring crops, improving agricultural efficiency.
Aerospace: Assist in assembling complex components and conducting maintenance tasks.
Research and Development: Facilitate experiments and prototype testing, enhancing innovation.

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning: Improve the adaptability and decision-making capabilities of robotic arms, enabling them to handle complex tasks with minimal human intervention.
Human-Robot Collaboration: Development of cobots (collaborative robots) that work safely alongside humans, enhancing productivity without replacing human workers.
Tactile Sensing and Haptics: Enable robotic arms to perform delicate tasks requiring fine motor skills and precise force control.
Modular and Reconfigurable Systems: Allow customization of robotic arms for specific tasks, providing flexibility in various industrial applications.
Teleoperation and Remote Control: Enable operation of robotic arms from remote locations, useful in hazardous environments and space exploration.
